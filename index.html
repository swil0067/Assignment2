Throughtout the world, COVID-19 vaccinations peaked from around April to October of 2021.

Vaccinations over Time in Australia

As shown above Australia begun vaccinating their population for COVID-19 before July of 2021 and reached 95% of the population vaccinated with the first dose by around February of 2022.

The vaccination roleout in Australia has been very successful with getting people vaccinated with their first and second doses, however there was a significant drop off in the percentage of people in Australia who decided to get their third dose.

When the perecntage of people who had recieved the third dose vaccination really started to plateau, around May of 2022, 7 million cases of COVID had already been recorded according to Worldometer. Without accounting for repeat cases, that is nearly 30% of the Australian population!

This ubiquity, as well as just getting out of the biggest peak of cases they would see throughout the pandemic likely made the third dose of the vaccine seem much less necessary than it is.

Increasing Vaccinations

There are a many ways to increase vaccination rates, however one very important one that has somewhat been implemented is donating vaccines. As the map above shows, the countries that have bought many times more doses than their populations require are all in the High Income tier.

For lower income countries, that cannot necessarily afford to vaccinate, especially fully vaccinate, their population and to therefore achieve herd immunity and protect everyone who has not or cannot get the vaccine, higher income countries have donated vaccines to lower income countries.
